\section{Outlier Detection}

\begin{frame}
    \frametitle{
    Outlier Detection
    \par\hspace{-0.73cm}
    \small{What is outlier detection?}}
    \vspace{-0.73cm}
    We assume that most of the time series within a database follow some distribution. If a time series diverges from this distribution significantly it is considered as outlier. We need a function, telling us something about this amount:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item A score $f: X \times I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}_+$ can be computed directly.
        \item A score $f \circ g_i: X \times I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}_+$ can be computed on overlapping fixed sized windows, where $g_i: X \times I \rightarrow U \times J$ for $U \subset X$ and $J \subset I$ and $\{g_i\}_{i \in I}$ covers $X \times I$.
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
