\caption{Gray {\color{gray}$\bullet$} signatures are valid. Yellow {\color{lightgold}$\bullet$} signatures are critical and stop interpolation. Red {\color{darkcandy}$\bullet$} signatures are invalid. Persistence diagrams are calculated on the Delaunay complex. In the first letter of $(0)$ the natural neighbor is more often the point to the right of the writing than the viewer's line of thought, implying an \textit{N}. The interpolation stops when the topology of these points creates new connected components, see top row $(2)$. In the last part of the writing is an element of $H_1$ which disappears. It is noticeable that many holes are preserved, but the density of the points is changed and certain holes are shifted, like the distance of a \textit{V}-forming letter pair. This shift creates differences in the persistence diagrams and also leads to the stop.}
